Felix Delgado-Ferro

Felix Delgado Ferro is a PhD Student at the Department of Signal Theory, Telematics and Communications of the University of Granada. His research focuses on wireless networks and mobile communications, particularly in the context of IoT and beyond 5G/6G systems.

He received the B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Telecommunication Engineering from the University of Granada, Spain, in 2020 and 2022, respectively. He is currently pursuing the Ph.D. degree in Telecommunication Engineering with the Wireless and Multimedia Networking Lab Research Group.

In 2023, he was a visiting doctoral researcher at Simula Research Laboratory, University of Oslo, Norway.

Education

  • Ph.D. in Telecommunication Engineering (ongoing) University of Granada
  • M.Sc. in Telecommunication Engineering (2022) University of Granada

Research

He is a member of the Wireless and Multimedia Networking Lab (TIC-235), where he participates in several research projects related to IoT, LPWAN, and 5G/6G networks.

  • 5G/6G mobile networks and network slicing
  • IoT and LPWAN (LoRaWAN)
  • Multipath protocols and heterogeneous networks
  • AI/ML for network optimization

For more details, please visit the Research section.

Academic Activities

He is actively involved in teaching and disseminating activities within the Telematics Engineering area at the University of Granada, contributing to both undergraduate and postgraduate programs.

He also collaborates in research supervision and academic projects related to advanced communication systems (Bachelor and Master theses).

More information can be found in the Teaching section.
